Introduction to ModBus

Modbus is a widely used communication protocol in the field of industrial automation and control systems. It was developed by Modicon (now Schneider Electric) in 1979 and has since become a de facto standard for interconnecting industrial electronic devices. The protocol is known for its simplicity, versatility, and robustness, making it suitable for various applications ranging from simple data acquisition to complex distributed control systems.

Modbus operates on a master-slave architecture, where a master device (such as a programmable logic controller or PLC) communicates with one or more slave devices (sensors, actuators, etc.). The communication can be either serial (RS-232 or RS-485) or Ethernet-based (Modbus TCP/IP).

The protocol supports different data types and functions for reading and writing data, such as coils (digital outputs), discrete inputs (digital inputs), holding registers (analog values), and input registers (read-only analog values).

Overall, Modbus plays a crucial role in industrial automation by enabling seamless communication between diverse devices, facilitating data exchange, monitoring, and control within industrial environments.
